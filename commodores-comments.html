Commodore's Comments

Recent Commodore’s Comments and archive links for club news while older entries are still being migrated.

We are into the wet weather phase of winter here in the Bay Area, and it is time to think about what you want to do this year in sailing, whether that means improving skills, getting better race results, attending more regattas, or finally fixing that boat issue you found last year.

Winter is a good time to finish boat projects, from new sails and woodwork to lines, blocks, trailer work, and other long-postponed maintenance. The club has a lot of expertise, so ask for help and advice when you need it.

If improving your skill level is the goal, the club’s Zoom meetings are a good place to get tips on going faster, ask tactical questions, learn more about boat setup, and sharpen your understanding of the sailing rules.

Additional online learning resources mentioned in the current comment include Speed & Smarts and the US Sailing store for books and rules guidance.

And one very practical reminder: check on your boat. If it is stored outdoors, make sure it is covered securely and dry inside before spring sailing ramps back up.
